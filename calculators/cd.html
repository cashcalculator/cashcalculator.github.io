Understanding Certificates of Deposit

A Certificate of Deposit (CD) is a savings account that holds a fixed amount of money for a fixed period of time. In exchange for leaving your money untouched, the bank pays you interest at a guaranteed rate.

APY vs APR: APY (Annual Percentage Yield) accounts for compound interest, while APR does not. APY gives you a more accurate picture of your actual returns.
